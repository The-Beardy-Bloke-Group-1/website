About the Book

Volume One – A Collection of 25 Poems

Poetry by a Beardy Bloke is not your typical poetry book. Inside, you’ll find a mix of humour, vulnerability and raw honesty. Mark Webb writes in a way that is accessible, emotional and unafraid to touch on difficult topics that many people avoid.

The book includes 25 original poems exploring mental health, masculinity, identity, toxic behaviours, relationships, childhood memories, pets, humour and the messy parts of everyday life.

At the back of the book, Mark provides a unique insight into each poem — why he wrote it, where he was in life at the time, and what message he hoped readers would take away.

What’s Inside the Book?

Real Topics, Real Emotion

The collection balances heavy and light themes, allowing readers to laugh at one moment and reflect deeply the next.

  • ✔ Mental health & emotional wellbeing
  • ✔ Masculinity & identity
  • ✔ Alcohol, coping and difficult choices
  • ✔ Relationships, heartbreak & honesty
  • ✔ Pets, humour & everyday randomness
  • ✔ Growing up & understanding yourself

Whether you're a poetry fan or not, the book is intentionally written to be relatable, impactful and easy to read.


Featured Poems

The Man I Pretend To Be

A reflective piece exploring the gap between who we are and who we pretend to be. This poem sparks powerful discussions about emotional honesty, especially among young men.

Unicorns Are Real

A fun and imaginative poem that uses humour to encourage creativity, optimism and escapism — proving that poetry doesn’t always need to be serious.

Getting It Up

A comedic take on confidence, pressure and expectations. Behind the humour lies a deeper message about body image and self-worth.

Cats, Dogs & Other Companions

A warm, relatable poem celebrating pets as emotional anchors in chaotic lives.

The Bottle and Me

A raw and honest look at how people use alcohol to cope with stress and emotional struggle.


Why I Wrote This Book

The Story Behind the Poetry

Mark started writing poetry because he struggled to express his emotions in conversation or through journaling. Poetry became a way to process his thoughts, experiences and the realities of working in a large inner-city college.

What began as a personal outlet quickly became something others connected to — friends, family, students, colleagues and eventually complete strangers.

The book exists to spark conversations, challenge stereotypes and show that vulnerability is not weakness — it’s connection.
